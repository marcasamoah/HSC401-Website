The Eyes

In the eyes, the parasympathetic divison of contracts the sphinchter muscle controlling the pupil (acting on its MAChRs), making it smaller. It also makes the lens more convex, allowing for closer vision.

In, contrast, the sympathetic division acts on the α1 adrenergic receptors of the radial muscle controlling the pupil, making it wider (by contracting the muscle). It also acts on β2 adrenergic receptors to flatten the lens, allowing for far vision. Try clicking the buttons to toggle the effects of each division.
