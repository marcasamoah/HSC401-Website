Structure

As shown in the in the overview, parasympathetic preganglionic neurons originate from cranial nerves III (the ocular motor) VII (the facial) IX (the glossopharyngeal), and X (the vagus) at the brainstem or the sacral region of the spinal cord. These neurons synapse with postganglionic neurons near, or inside the wall of, their target organs.
